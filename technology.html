the terminology...

launch vehicle

A launch vehicle or carrier rocket is a rocket-propelled
vehicle used to carry a payload from Earth's surface
to space, usually to Earth orbit or beyond. Our WEB-X
carrier rocket is the most powerful in operation.
Standing 150 metres tall, it's quite an awe-inspiring
sight on the launch pad!

Spaceport

A spaceport or cosmodrome is a site for launching (or receiving) spacecraft, by analogy to the seaport
for ships or airport for aircraft. Based in
the famous Cape Canaveral, our spaceport is
ideally situated to take advantage of the Earthâ€™s
rotation for launch.

Space capsule

A space capsule is an often-crewed spacecraft
that uses a blunt-body reentry capsule to reenter
the Earth's atmosphere without wings. Our capsule is
where you'll spend your time during the flight.
It includes a space gym, cinema, and plenty of
other activities to keep you entertained.
